Seattle Lavender Farm Family & Maternity Sessions

Seasonal lavender farm photos in Sequim for families and expecting parents who want soft color, open fields, and connection-focused portraits.

Book a Lavender Session View Lavender Gallery →

Lavender farm sessions are offered during a short mid-July bloom window, when the rows of purple flowers, green stems, open space, and warm evening light create a look you cannot get year-round.

This is the option to choose when the setting itself feels worth building a day around. It is not the quickest or closest way to update your photos, but that is part of what makes the final images feel different from a regular park or field.

The lavender is the reason to make the trip, but it is not the main subject. These photos are still about your child's expressions, the way your kids move through this age, the connection between you, or the quiet anticipation of pregnancy before baby arrives.

Choose Your Setting Pace

Two options are available: a shorter mini and a fuller deluxe. Both take place at a private farm in Sequim during the mid-July bloom window, and both can be used for family or maternity photos.

Choose the mini session if you want a shorter lavender-field session and know your family does best with an efficient plan. Choose the deluxe session if you are making a special trip and want more time, more variety, and a more complete experience at the farm.

Because the farm is in Sequim, please plan for the longer drive and leave extra buffer before your start time. This session is best for families who are comfortable making the trip for a more distinctive seasonal setting.

I will help coordinate wardrobe suggestions that look beautiful against the soft violet hues (neutrals, soft creams, warm linen, and muted pastels look best).

COMMON QUESTIONS

Lavender Farm FAQ

Everything you need to know about preparing for our seasonal lavender field sessions.

Where exactly are these sessions located?

+

They take place at a private, beautifully maintained lavender farm in Sequim on the Olympic Peninsula. It requires a scenic ferry ride or drive from Seattle, making for a perfect day-trip adventure.

When is the lavender in bloom?

+

The lavender has a very narrow peak bloom window, typically from early July through late July. Sessions are scheduled during the middle two weeks of July when color is most vibrant.

What happens if it rains?

+

Summers on the Olympic Peninsula are generally very dry, but if we encounter significant rain, we will reschedule. If we can't find a mutually agreeable date, your deposit is refunded.

Can we do both family and maternity photos?

+

Absolutely! The lavender fields are a stunning backdrop for maternity, family, couples, or children's milestone portraits. Styling is tailored to your session type.

Are props allowed on the farm?

+

We keep styling natural and organic to match the scenery. Simple items like blankets, picnic baskets, or sun hats are perfect; large artificial props are discouraged to preserve the natural aesthetic.
